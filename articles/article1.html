# Clinical Case Study: Hypertension – Evidence-Based Management and Pharmacist Intervention **Author:** Dr. K. Nandhakumar, Pharm.D **Published:** 2026 **Category:** Clinical Pharmacy | Cardiovascular Disorders | Case Study ## Introduction Hypertension is one of the most common chronic cardiovascular disorders worldwide and a major risk factor for stroke, myocardial infarction, heart failure, chronic kidney disease, and premature mortality. Effective blood pressure control requires a combination of pharmacological therapy, lifestyle modification, regular monitoring, and patient education. Clinical pharmacists play a crucial role in optimizing antihypertensive therapy, identifying drug-related problems, improving medication adherence, and supporting evidence-based clinical decision-making. --- ## Patient Information **Age:** 55 years **Gender:** Male **Weight:** 78 kg **Height:** 170 cm ### Chief Complaint The patient presented with persistent elevated blood pressure readings despite receiving antihypertensive therapy for the past six months. ### Medical History * Hypertension diagnosed 5 years ago * Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus for 3 years * Sedentary lifestyle * Family history of cardiovascular disease ### Current Medication * Amlodipine 5 mg once daily --- ## Clinical Assessment ### Vital Signs | Parameter | Value | | ---------------- | ------------ | | Blood Pressure | 168/102 mmHg | | Heart Rate | 84 bpm | | Respiratory Rate | 18/min | | Temperature | 98.6°F | ### Laboratory Findings | Investigation | Result | | ------------------- | --------- | | Fasting Blood Sugar | 148 mg/dL | | HbA1c | 7.8% | | Serum Creatinine | 1.0 mg/dL | | Total Cholesterol | 225 mg/dL | | LDL Cholesterol | 145 mg/dL | ### Assessment The patient's blood pressure remained uncontrolled despite monotherapy with amlodipine. Additional cardiovascular risk factors included diabetes mellitus, dyslipidemia, obesity, and physical inactivity. --- ## Drug Therapy Problems Identified 1. Uncontrolled hypertension. 2. Inadequate response to monotherapy. 3. Increased cardiovascular risk. 4. Poor adherence to lifestyle modifications. 5. Need for regular blood pressure monitoring. --- ## Evidence-Based Management Current hypertension guidelines recommend combination therapy when blood pressure remains uncontrolled with a single antihypertensive agent. ### Pharmacological Intervention The treating physician initiated: * Amlodipine 5 mg once daily (continued) * Ramipril 5 mg once daily (added) ### Rationale #### Amlodipine * Calcium channel blocker * Effective in reducing peripheral vascular resistance * Provides sustained blood pressure control #### Ramipril * Angiotensin-Converting Enzyme (ACE) inhibitor * Improves cardiovascular outcomes * Offers renal protection in diabetic patients --- ## Lifestyle Modification Plan The patient received counseling regarding: ### Dietary Recommendations * Reduced sodium intake (<2 g/day) * Increased consumption of fruits and vegetables * Reduced saturated fat intake * Adequate hydration ### Physical Activity * Brisk walking for 30–45 minutes daily * At least 150 minutes of moderate exercise per week ### Weight Management * Target BMI within normal range * Calorie-controlled diet ### Other Recommendations * Smoking cessation * Limitation of alcohol consumption * Stress management techniques * Regular follow-up visits --- ## Pharmacist Intervention Clinical pharmacist services included: ### Medication Review * Assessed appropriateness of therapy * Evaluated potential adverse effects * Reviewed possible drug interactions ### Patient Counseling * Explained disease progression and complications * Emphasized medication adherence * Educated on home blood pressure monitoring ### Monitoring Plan * Weekly blood pressure assessment * Monitoring for dizziness, hypotension, and cough * Periodic renal function evaluation ### Documentation All interventions and recommendations were documented in the patient's clinical records and communicated to the healthcare team. --- ## Follow-Up Outcome After eight weeks of therapy: | Parameter | Initial | Follow-Up | | -------------------- | ------------ | ----------- | | Blood Pressure | 168/102 mmHg | 128/82 mmHg | | Medication Adherence | Poor | Excellent | | Lifestyle Compliance | Low | Improved | The patient demonstrated significant improvement in blood pressure control and overall adherence to treatment recommendations. --- ## Discussion Combination antihypertensive therapy is frequently required to achieve target blood pressure goals, particularly in patients with multiple cardiovascular risk factors. ACE inhibitors and calcium channel blockers have demonstrated effectiveness in reducing cardiovascular morbidity and mortality. Clinical pharmacists contribute substantially to patient outcomes through medication optimization, adherence support, therapeutic monitoring, and evidence-based recommendations. --- ## Conclusion This case highlights the importance of evidence-based hypertension management and multidisciplinary healthcare collaboration. The addition of an ACE inhibitor, combined with lifestyle modification and pharmacist-led interventions, resulted in successful blood pressure control and improved patient outcomes. Clinical pharmacists remain essential members of the healthcare team in achieving optimal management of hypertension and preventing long-term cardiovascular complications. --- ### Learning Points * Uncontrolled hypertension often requires combination therapy. * Lifestyle modification is a cornerstone of hypertension management. * Clinical pharmacists improve medication adherence and therapeutic outcomes. * Regular monitoring helps prevent complications and optimize treatment. * Evidence-based interventions significantly reduce cardiovascular risk. **Keywords:** Hypertension, Clinical Pharmacy, Pharmacist Intervention, Evidence-Based Medicine, Cardiovascular Disease, Patient Counseling, Drug Therapy Management.
