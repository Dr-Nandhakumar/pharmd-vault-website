Clinical Case Study: Hypertension – Evidence-Based Management and Pharmacist Intervention

Author: Dr. K. Nandhakumar, Pharm.D
Published: 2026
Category: Clinical Pharmacy | Cardiovascular Disorders | Case Study

Introduction

Hypertension is one of the most common chronic cardiovascular disorders worldwide and a major risk factor for stroke, myocardial infarction, heart failure, chronic kidney disease, and premature mortality.

Effective blood pressure control requires a combination of pharmacological therapy, lifestyle modification, regular monitoring, and patient education.

Clinical pharmacists play a crucial role in optimizing antihypertensive therapy, identifying drug-related problems, improving medication adherence, and supporting evidence-based clinical decision-making.

Patient Information

  • Age: 55 years
  • Gender: Male
  • Weight: 78 kg
  • Height: 170 cm

Chief Complaint

The patient presented with persistent elevated blood pressure readings despite receiving antihypertensive therapy for the past six months.

Medical History

  • Hypertension diagnosed 5 years ago
  • Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus for 3 years
  • Sedentary lifestyle
  • Family history of cardiovascular disease

Current Medication

  • Amlodipine 5 mg once daily

Clinical Assessment

Vital Signs

Parameter Value
Blood Pressure 168/102 mmHg
Heart Rate 84 bpm
Respiratory Rate 18/min
Temperature 98.6°F

Laboratory Findings

Investigation Result
Fasting Blood Sugar 148 mg/dL
HbA1c 7.8%
Serum Creatinine 1.0 mg/dL
Total Cholesterol 225 mg/dL
LDL Cholesterol 145 mg/dL

Assessment

The patient's blood pressure remained uncontrolled despite monotherapy with amlodipine. Additional cardiovascular risk factors included diabetes mellitus, dyslipidemia, obesity, and physical inactivity.

Drug Therapy Problems Identified

  1. Uncontrolled hypertension.
  2. Inadequate response to monotherapy.
  3. Increased cardiovascular risk.
  4. Poor adherence to lifestyle modifications.
  5. Need for regular blood pressure monitoring.

Evidence-Based Management

Current hypertension guidelines recommend combination therapy when blood pressure remains uncontrolled with a single antihypertensive agent.

Pharmacological Intervention

  • Amlodipine 5 mg once daily (continued)
  • Ramipril 5 mg once daily (added)

Lifestyle Modification Plan

  • Reduced sodium intake (<2 g/day)
  • Increased consumption of fruits and vegetables
  • Regular physical activity
  • Weight management
  • Smoking cessation
  • Stress management

Pharmacist Intervention

  • Medication review
  • Patient counselling
  • Drug interaction assessment
  • Home blood pressure monitoring education
  • Periodic renal function monitoring

Follow-Up Outcome

Parameter Initial Follow-Up
Blood Pressure 168/102 mmHg 128/82 mmHg
Medication Adherence Poor Excellent
Lifestyle Compliance Low Improved

The patient demonstrated significant improvement in blood pressure control and overall adherence to treatment recommendations.

Discussion

Combination antihypertensive therapy is frequently required to achieve target blood pressure goals, particularly in patients with multiple cardiovascular risk factors.

Clinical pharmacists contribute substantially to patient outcomes through medication optimization, adherence support, therapeutic monitoring, and evidence-based recommendations.

Conclusion

This case highlights the importance of evidence-based hypertension management and multidisciplinary healthcare collaboration.

Learning Points

  • Uncontrolled hypertension often requires combination therapy.
  • Lifestyle modification is a cornerstone of hypertension management.
  • Clinical pharmacists improve medication adherence and therapeutic outcomes.
  • Regular monitoring helps prevent complications.
  • Evidence-based interventions reduce cardiovascular risk.
